## What FuseMax did

**Precision config**: 16-bit fixed throughout. Architecture YAML pins multiplier_width=16, adder_width=16 for every PE. FP units = 8b exp / 8b mantissa (FP16). No separate K/V-cache precision — one datatype across logits, softmax exp, accumulation, and stored K/V. **NVFP4, MXFP4, FP8, FP6 are NOT evaluated.**

**Compiler/HW split**: Pure HW + dataflow design. "Cascade of Einsums" is a mathematical abstraction (TeAAL-based), NOT a programmable compiler or codegen. Designs are described as Einsum specs, mapped/bound by HAND to a fixed spatial array. Modeled with Timeloop + Accelergy. **No DSL emits RTL or vector ISA code.**

**Target HW**: 2D 256×256 + 1D 256 spatial PE mesh. One fpmac + one max-compare + sequential mac_exponential per PE. 940 MHz, 16nm (TPUv3-like timing); 45nm Accelergy/Aladdin for area/energy. HBM2 DRAM, 32-bank SRAM global buffer. No mm² or W reported, only relative ratios.

**Comparisons**: FLAT (primary, iso-area), FlashAttention-2, TileFlow, E.T., PyTorch/TF, Rabe&Staats, Choi et al. Result: **6.7× speedup vs FLAT at 79% energy** (attention), **5.3× end-to-end at 83% energy**.

**Workloads**: BERT, TrXL, T5, XLM (encoder/decoder-encoder transformers). Target "1M tokens and beyond". **No Llama, no llama.cpp, no autoregressive decoder LLM, no KV-cache growth evaluation.**

## Differentiation lines for our paper

The Y1 paper write-up can lead with:
1. "FuseMax shows attention fusion matters; we extend to **mixed precision** routing per stage."
2. "FuseMax uses fixed Einsum dataflow descriptions; we add **compiler co-design via Exo @instr** binding to real RVV custom instructions."
3. "FuseMax targets a 256×256 systolic mesh; we target **RVV vector + Saturn** with VLEN-parameterized scheduling."
4. "FuseMax evaluates on encoder transformers (BERT/T5); we evaluate **autoregressive decoder LLMs** (Llama-3.2-1B) where KV-cache memory bandwidth is the bottleneck."
5. "FuseMax pins all stages to FP16; we route **BF16 logits / NVFP4 K/V cache / FP8 softmax / BF16 output**."

## Strategic notes for Task 9 (precision config pin)

- FuseMax's all-FP16 result gives us a clear baseline to beat on memory bandwidth (4× via NVFP4 K/V) while staying accurate.
- IEEE Micro 2025 retrospective adds **zero** new precision evaluation — we are not late to a mixed-precision FuseMax extension.
- The "extended Einsums" framework is descriptive — meaning precision is a "free variable" they didn't sweep. Our paper can frame: "FuseMax left the precision dimension unexplored; we route precisions per Einsum-stage via Exo @instr."
- ARCQuant (arXiv:2601.07475) is the recent NVFP4-for-LLM algorithm paper — cite as the "algorithm baseline that motivates HW support."
