## Why this framing

- "Side project I'm scoping" — honest, doesn't overcommit, signals you'll share more later.
- Specifies the workload (Llama-3.2-1B decode + KV-cache bandwidth) so they know it's not exotic and matches the LLM-arch + compiler-co-design space the advisor already works in. Hints at the real research direction without pitching.
- Lists multiple acceptable answers (SiFive / SpacemiT / FireSim) so they don't get stuck looking for the exact item.
- Time-bounded ask (~6 months) — not indefinite borrow.
- "Happy to share once concrete" — softly opens the door to the eventual project pitch without forcing it.

## What NOT to include (yet)

- The MLArchSys 2027 submission target.
- The mixed-precision NVFP4 K/V cache + Exo precision-routing pass + four custom RVV instructions plan. Defer to the full pitch after Mo 4 RTL milestone, when you can show a working `vfexp` Chisel module + area numbers + a precision-routing demo.
- Any mention of PhD applications.
- Budget/cost framing — borrowing hardware is normal lab practice, not a financial ask.
- The Saturn microarch advantage angle (chaining, scoreboard, OoO) — this is a positioning hook for the full pitch, premature here.
