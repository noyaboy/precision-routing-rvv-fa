**Mo 6 framing risks surfaced by J3** (worth thinking about before Track J-2):

1. **Compute-bound at small scale.** At seq_len = 2048, head_dim = 64 the BF16 RVV row-major path runs at 0.22 B/cyc DRAM, well within DDR3-1600's 6.4 B/cyc capacity. The NVFP4 BW advantage doesn't dominate cycles until the workload outgrows L2 or the simulator models the real Saturn FU latency. Likely need to extend Mo 6 measurements to seq_len ∈ {8192, 32768} and head_dim ≥ 128 to expose the BW advantage at the µarch level.
2. **Scalar NVFP4 dequant is the gem5 floor.** Real Saturn FU absorbs dequant in vector pipe at 16-lane width; gem5 can't issue Saturn's custom encodings. J3+stub (59.73 M cyc) is the closest projection on this simulator. The integration number on a Saturn-µarch-aware O3 simulator (e.g. when FireSim FPGA path is added) will be lower.
3. **Mo 6 ≥1.5× depends on SpacemiT-K1-ported cycle count.** If port lands above ~90 M cyc (likely because the upstream is VLEN=1024-hardcoded and degrades at VLEN=256), J3+stub meets ≥1.5×. If port lands at ~60 M (matches BF16-RVV equivalent), Mo 6 needs the long-context extension.
